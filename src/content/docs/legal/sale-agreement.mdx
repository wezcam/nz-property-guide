When you sell your home privately, you do not manage the legal exchange yourself. You leave that entirely to the professionals.

In New Zealand, virtually every property transaction uses the official, standardized **ADLS/REINZ Sale and Purchase Agreement**. You do not need to understand every piece of legal jargon inside it—you just need to follow one absolute golden rule.

---

## The Golden Rule: Lawyer to Lawyer

<Aside title="Never Sign Anything First" type="danger">
  Never sign a written offer or a Sale & Purchase Agreement the moment a buyer hands it to you. A verbal offer means nothing in NZ property law, and a signature locks you in completely. Always keep it professional and say: *"Thank you, please have your solicitor send the formal offer straight to my lawyer."*
</Aside>

### The Direct Workflow

Without a real estate agent acting as the middleman, the contract follows a straight line between the legal professionals:

```text
[Buyer's Lawyer] ─── Sends Written Offer ───► [Your Conveyancing Lawyer]
                                                        │
                                               Reviews & Safe Checks
                                                        │
                                                        ▼
                                             [You (Sign or Counter)]

1. THE BUYER DRAFTS
The buyer works with their lawyer to fill in the price, their deposit, 
and their conditions (like finance or a building report) using the 
template from your Pre-Prepared Contract Pack.

2. THE LEGAL EXCHANGE
The buyer's lawyer emails the formal document directly to your 
conveyancing solicitor.

3. THE PROFESSIONAL REVIEW
Your lawyer calls you, reviews the terms over the phone, ensures your 
upfront disclosures are fully protected, and advises you whether to 
sign it or counter-offer.

By keeping the communication strictly lawyer-to-lawyer, you completely 
protect yourself legally while eliminating the stress of negotiating 
contract clauses directly with the buyer.
