Most homeowners think you only call a lawyer once you have found a buyer. **This is a critical mistake for private sellers.** 

If an eager buyer walks into your first open home on Sunday, loves the house, and wants to make an offer, you cannot let them go home and wait a week for paperwork to be generated. Momentum kills deals. You need to hand them a clean, legally sound contract pack on the spot.

---

## The Proactive Solicitor Protocol

Before your professional photographer even arrives, you need to call a local property lawyer or conveyancing practitioner. Let them know explicitly: *"I am selling my home privately, and I want to set up a Pre-Prepared Contract Pack for my open homes."*

Your solicitor will immediately action three critical tasks for you:

1. **Pull the Certificate of Title:** They will download a copy of your Land Title from Land Information New Zealand (LINZ) to ensure there are no unexpected historic encumbrances or boundary flags that could scare off a buyer's bank.
2. **Review Your Mandatory Disclosures:** Under NZ law, you cannot intentionally hide structural flaws or unpermitted work. You must tell your lawyer about any leaky building history, non-consented DIY retaining walls, or broken chattels. Your lawyer will determine what needs to be legally disclosed to protect you from future lawsuits under the Contract and Commercial Law Act 2017.
3. **Bake Disclosures into the Contract:** Instead of giving buyers a completely blank slate, your lawyer will draft a custom ADLS/REINZ Sale and Purchase Agreement with your specific disclosures already neatly written into the **"Further Terms"** section on the back page.

---

## Why the "Pre-Prepared Pack" Changes Everything

When an interested buyer requests the paperwork, you don't send them to look for a generic form. You hand them (or email them a link to) your specific **Pre-Prepared Contract Pack**.

This turns the tables entirely in your favor:

* **It Minimizes Buyer Friction:** The buyer's lawyer doesn't have to spend hours writing custom clauses from scratch. They simply take your lawyer's pre-approved document, fill in their purchase price, deposit amount, and finance dates, sign it, and hand it back.
* **It Establishes Authority:** It signals to the market that you are completely prepared, legally protected, and acting with professional support—completely neutralizing any hesitation a buyer might have about dealing with a private individual.

<Aside type="tip" title="What to Include in Your Open Home Folder">
  Print out two physical copies for the kitchen bench, and have a digital Google Drive folder ready containing:
  * The pre-prepared Sale & Purchase Agreement (with your disclosures integrated).
  * A copy of the Land Title.
  * The most recent Council Rates Invoice.
  * Your professional floor plan layout.
</Aside>
