Most homeowners think you only call a lawyer once you have found a buyer. **This is a critical mistake for private sellers.** 

If an eager buyer walks into your first open home on Sunday, loves the house, and wants to make an offer, you cannot let them go home and wait weeks for paperwork to be generated. Momentum kills deals. You need to hand them a clean, legally sound contract pack on the spot.

---

## The Proactive Solicitor Protocol

Before your professional photographer even arrives, you need to call a local property lawyer or conveyancing practitioner. Let them know explicitly: *"I am selling my home privately, and I want to set up a Pre-Prepared Contract Pack for my open homes."*

Your solicitor will immediately action four critical tasks for you:

1. **Pull the Certificate of Title:** They will download a copy of your Land Title from LINZ to ensure there are no unexpected historic encumbrances or boundary flags that could scare off a buyer's bank.
2. **Order a Council LIM Report:** A Land Information Memorandum (LIM) reveals flood risks, boundaries, and council consents. While buyers usually order these, ordering it yourself up front is a massive tactical advantage (see below). Expect to pay between **\$350 and \$500** depending on your local city or district council.
3. **Review Your Mandatory Disclosures:** Under NZ law, you cannot intentionally hide structural flaws or unpermitted work. You must tell your lawyer about any leaky building history, non-consented DIY retaining walls, or broken chattels.
4. **Bake Disclosures into the Contract:** Your lawyer will draft a custom ADLS/REINZ Sale and Purchase Agreement with your specific disclosures neatly written into the **"Further Terms"** section on the back page.

---

## The Strategic Power of providing the LIM Up Front

Councils legally have up to **10 working days** to process a standard LIM request. If a buyer has to submit an offer conditional on ordering their own LIM, your house is effectively locked up and off the market for two full weeks while they wait for the council queue.

By paying for the LIM up front and putting it in your pack:
* **You Slash the Settlement Timeline:** Buyers can hand the LIM straight to their lawyer or bank immediately, reducing a standard 15-day conditional period down to a fast 5-day finance check.
* **You Build Instant Trust:** Providing a clean council history upfront proves you have absolutely nothing to hide, completely neutralizing the natural hesitation buyers sometimes feel with private sales.

<Aside type="tip" title="What to Include in Your Open Home Folder">
  Print out two physical copies for the kitchen bench, and have a digital Google Drive folder ready containing:
  * The pre-prepared Sale & Purchase Agreement (with your disclosures integrated).
  * The freshly issued Council LIM Report.
  * A copy of the Land Title.
  * The most recent Council Rates Invoice.
  * Your professional floor plan layout.
</Aside>
