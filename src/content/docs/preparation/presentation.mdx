First impressions dictate final sale prices. When buyers scroll through Trade Me, you have less than two seconds to catch their eye before they swipe past. 

Because you aren't paying a traditional agent, you have the budget to hire a professional photographer. However, a photographer can only capture what is in front of the lens. Your job is to stage the home so it feels spacious, aspirational, and ready to move into.

---

## The 3 Golden Rules of DIY Staging

You do not need to spend \$4,000 renting corporate furniture. You can achieve 90% of the same impact by ruthlessly applying three rules:

### 1. Depersonalise (The "Hotel" Standard)
Buyers need to envision *their* life in the house, not yours. 
* Remove all family photos, fridge magnets, kids' drawings, and quirky collectables.
* Hide personal toiletries, toothbrushes, bath mats, and bins.
* Aim for the clean, neutral look of a high-end Airbnb.

### 2. De-clutter to Create Scale
Clutter shrinks rooms. If a room has too much furniture, it tells the buyer the house lacks storage.
* Temporarily move excess armchairs, large coffee tables, or bulky storage units into a storage unit or a friend's garage.
* Clear kitchen benches completely, leaving out absolutely maximum two high-end appliances (like a designer kettle or a coffee machine).
* Keep wardrobes half-empty. Buyers open built-in wardrobes; if they are bursting at the seams, it signals "not enough storage space."

### 3. Light and Neutrality
Kiwi buyers love warm, sun-drenched homes. 
* Replace every dead lightbulb with warm-white LEDs.
* Open all curtains, blinds, and nets completely.
* Add white or neutral throw cushions and a light-colored rug to make dark lounge spaces pop.

## Room-by-Room Prep Checklist

Before your photographer arrives, run through this absolute minimum preparation list:

| Area | The "Must-Do" Action Items |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Street Appeal** | Mow lawns, trim edges, water-blast paths, hide wheelie bins, park all cars down the road. |
| **Kitchen** | Hide dish racks, cloths, soaps, and appliances. Place a fresh bowl of green apples or citrus on the bench. |
| **Bathrooms** | Put out fresh, unused, neatly folded white towels. Clean mirrors with glass cleaner. Hide all shampoo bottles. |
| **Master Bedroom** | Use crisp, ironed, neutral bed linen. Add a textured throw blanket at the foot of the bed. Clear bedside tables. |

## Managing Your Professional Photographer

Since you are managing the shoot directly, you are the creative director. When you hire a local real estate photographer, explicitly instruct them to deliver these three core assets:

1. **Wide-Angle Interior Shots:** Taken from waist-height to make rooms look as open and expansive as possible.
2. **A Clear Floor Plan:** **Crucial.** Trade Me listings with a floor plan get significantly higher engagement because buyers need to understand the flow before booking an open home.
3. **The "Hero" Exterior Shot:** Dusk photography (the "golden hour") works exceptionally well in New Zealand to make properties look warm and high-end.

<Aside type="tip" title="Weather Check">
  If your booking lands on a classic overcast or rainy Kiwi day, ask your photographer if they include "blue sky replacement" for the exterior shots. Most professional real estate editors can swap a grey sky for a sunny one in post-production for free.
</Aside>
