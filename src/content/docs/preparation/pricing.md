The biggest mistake private sellers make is pricing their home based on emotion, what they paid for it, or what they *need* to buy their next house. Buyers don't care about any of those things—they only care about current market reality.

Because you aren't paying a real estate agent a \$30,000 commission, you have a massive competitive advantage. You can price your home sharply and cleanly. Here is the exact framework to find your magic number.

---

## Step 1: Gather Your Free Digital Data

Before paying for professional valuations, pull the public data that buyers will be looking at. In New Zealand, three core platforms drive consumer expectations:

1. **Homes.co.nz:** Look up your address to see your "HomesEstimate." Note the estimated range (e.g., \$820k – \$890k). 
2. **OneRoof.co.nz:** Check their automated valuation model (AVM). It often uses different algorithms than Homes.co.nz.
3. **QV.co.nz (Quotable Value):** Look up your property's latest Capital Value (RV/CV). 

<Aside type="caution" title="Important About CV/RV">
  Your local Council Rating Value (CV or RV) is for **tax purposes**, not market value. If your council did valuations during the peak of the market, your CV might be higher than what the house is worth today. If they did it years ago, it will be too low. Never rely solely on CV.
</Aside>

---

## Step 2: Build a CMA (Comparative Market Analysis)

Buyers judge the value of your home by comparing it to houses they looked at last weekend. You need to do the exact same thing. 

Go to the "Sold" tabs on Trade Me Property, OneRoof, and Realestate.co.nz. Find **3 to 5 properties** that meet these criteria:
*   Located within your immediate suburb (ideally within a 1–2km radius).
*   Sold within the **last 3 to 6 months** (do not look at older data, as the NZ market shifts quickly).
*   Similar layout (e.g., 3 bedrooms, 2 bathrooms, standalone brick and tile).

Create a simple spreadsheet comparing their land size, floor area, condition, and final sale price against your own home.

---

## Step 3: Factor in the "Private Sale Discount" (Your Secret Weapon)

When buyers see a private sale on Trade Me, they automatically expect a slightly better deal because they know you aren't paying an agent. 

Let's look at the math:

| Feature | Traditional Agent Sale | Your Private Sale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Target Market Value** | \$1,000,000 | \$1,000,000 |
| **Agent Commission (incl. GST)** | -\$31,500 *(approx 3% tiered)* | \$0 |
| **Marketing Fees** | -\$2,500 | -\$799 *(Premium Trade Me Pack)* |
| **Net Cash in Your Pocket** | **\$966,000** | **\$999,201** |

**The Strategy:** By passing just **\$5,000 to \$10,000** of those savings onto the buyer by pricing your home slightly below the local "agent-led" competition, you will trigger an absolute flood of buyer inquiry, while still walking away with **\$20,000+ more profit** than if you used an agency.

---

## Step 4: Choose Your Pricing Strategy

When you list on Trade Me, you must choose how to display the price. In New Zealand, three methods work best for private sellers:

### 1. "Enquiries Over \$X" (Recommended)
Set the price roughly 5% below your target number. If you want \$850,000, list it as *"Enquiries Over \$819,000"*. This gets your house listed in lower search brackets, driving massive foot traffic to your first open home.

### 2. Fixed BEO (Buyer Enquiry Over)
A firm baseline price. Tell the market exactly what the minimum acceptable starting point is.

### 3. By Negotiation
Best if your property is truly unique or luxury, making it hard to find comparable sales data. 

<Aside type="danger" title="Avoid Tenders or Auctions">
  As a private seller, avoid running an Auction or a formal Tender process unless you are hiring a professional independent auctioneer. Buyers are already slightly hesitant about private sales; adding the high-stress legal pressure of an auction room without an agent mediating usually scares them away.
</Aside>
