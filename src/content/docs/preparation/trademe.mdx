In New Zealand, you don't need a real estate agency to get your home in front of buyers. Over 90% of Kiwi property buyers look for their next home on **Trade Me Property**. 

If your listing looks professional and your open homes are well-organized, buyers will judge the home on its merits—not on whether an agent opened the door. Here is your operational blueprint for going live.

---

## 1. Choosing Your Trade Me Private Sale Pack

Trade Me charges upfront flat fees for private sellers. Do not choose the cheapest basic listing; you want maximum visibility during your critical first two weeks on the market. 

| Feature Tier | Direct Cost (Approx) | Visibility Level | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic** | \$300 – \$500 | Low (Drops quickly in search) | Low-demand rural sections |
| **Standard** | \$600 – \$900 | Medium (Includes standard search card) | Steady, predictable markets |
| **Premium** | \$1,200 – \$1,600 | High (Top of search results, largest photo preview) | Maximum impact in main suburbs |

*Note: Pricing scales based on Trade Me's dynamic council valuation brackets for your region.*

**The Pro Move:** Invest in the **Premium Pack**. Since you saved close to \$30,000 on agent fees, allocating a fraction of that to stay pinned to the top of regional search results is the highest-yielding investment you can make.

## 2. Writing an Ad Copy That Sells

Don't write a boring list of features. Lead with the **lifestyle narrative** that fits your target buyer, followed by crisp bullet points.

### The Anatomy of a High-Conversion Private Ad:
*   **The Headline:** Make it specific. Instead of *"Beautiful 3 Bedroom House"*, use *"Sun-Drenched Brick & Tile | Flat Walk to the Beach"*.
*   **The Hook:** Start with who the house is for. *"Perfect for young professionals, active downsizers, or small families looking for absolute turnkey convenience..."*
*   **The Spec List:** Clearly list the vitals: floor area ($m^2$), land size ($m^2$), heating solutions (e.g., fully ducted heat pump), insulation compliance, and school zones.
*   **The Legal Callout:** End with total transparency: *"Pre-Prepared Contract Pack and clean Title available immediately via email or at the open home."*

## 3. The Open Home Playbook

Your first open home should run on a **Saturday and Sunday for 45 minutes each** (e.g., 11:00 AM – 11:45 AM). 

### Setup Checklist (1 Hour Before):
*   **Aromas:** Bake fresh cinnamon rolls, brew coffee, or use a light, clean room mist. Hide rubbish bins.
*   **Climate Control:** Turn on heat pumps or open windows early to ensure the indoor temperature sits perfectly between 18°C and 20°C.
*   **The Welcome Station:** Set up your kitchen bench with your printed floor plans, your **Pre-Prepared Contract Pack**, and a tablet or sign-in sheet.

## 4. Safety & Security Protocol

Hosting open homes privately is highly secure if you establish firm operational boundaries from minute one.

<Aside title="Safety Mandatory Rules" type="danger">
  Never let strangers freely wander your home unchecked. You are in control of the door. Follow these parameters strictly:
</Aside>

```text
               THE SECURE OPEN HOME WORKFLOW
               
    [Front Door Closed] ──► [Buyer Rings Bell / Knocks]
                                     │
                                     ▼
                        [Step 1: The Gatekeeper]
                        Mandatory Sign-in Required 
                        (Name, Mobile, Photo ID Check)
                                     │
                                     ▼
                        [Step 2: Flow Control]
                        Max 3 groups inside at once.
                        Others wait outside nicely.
                                     │
                                     ▼
                        [Step 3: Secure Property]
                        All cash, jewelry, and devices
                        locked completely away out of sight.
```
1. Mandatory Sign-In: Position yourself at the front door. Do not let people enter until they write down their full name, verified mobile number, and email. Simply state: "Hi there! For building insurance and vendor security, we require all visitors to sign in before viewing. Thank you!"

2. Control the Crowd Flow: If a wave of 20 people arrives at once, tell latecomers: "We are currently at capacity inside to give everyone a clear look. If you don't mind waiting 2 minutes on the deck, I'll bring your group straight through next!"

3. Valuables Lockdown: Before opening the doors, lock all small electronics, prescription medications, jewelry, passports, and loose house keys into a hidden home safe or remove them from the property entirely.
